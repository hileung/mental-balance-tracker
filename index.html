Posture Protocol

Body-Mind Link Use your body to stabilize your mind

Goal: Use posture as a fast, reliable way to tell your brain “I am stable enough to respond.”

Reset posture Doorway protocol
Physical setup: Feet shoulder-width (grounded), spine lengthened (not rigid), chest open (not puffed), shoulders soft (not hiked), jaw unclenched. Effect: Signals safety to nervous system, increases HRV, enables cognitive flexibility. Takes 30–60 seconds to shift autonomic state.
Cue Doorways, chair edges
Frequency 3–10 times / day
Detect patterns Collapsed vs braced
Collapsed (forward head, rounded shoulders): Signals defeat, increases rumination. Notice this → gently lengthen spine. Braced (jaw tight, shoulders hiked): Signals threat vigilance, narrows attention. Notice this → soften jaw, drop shoulders 1 cm.
Collapsed cue “Lengthen spine”
Braced cue “Drop shoulders, soften jaw”
Practice
Pick one environment (e.g. entering patient rooms) as your posture practice ground. Every threshold becomes a mini “equanimity checkpoint.”

Recovery sequence
  1. 1. Detect (proprioception): Notice you've been knocked off-center (“I'm tipping into anger”).
  2. 2. Pause (compensatory step): Interrupt the momentum—breath, posture reset, neutral phrase.
  3. 3. Control velocity: Prevent runaway thoughts/feelings—label story, let it pass without feeding it.
  4. 4. Re-center: Return attention to breath/body, reconnect to values (“Why am I here? To reduce suffering.”), resume action.
Tracking focus
Track your recovery speed weekly—this is your primary progress metric. Over months, you want: same number of perturbations, faster returns to a stable, values-aligned center.
