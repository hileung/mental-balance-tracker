Posture Protocol

Physical-Mental Link Stance as mental reset

Goal: Use your body as a fast access point to equanimity.

Stance Setup
Your physical posture directly signals your brain about your mental state. Use this bidirectional link deliberately.

Physical setup: Feet shoulder-width (grounded), spine lengthened (not rigid), chest open (not puffed), shoulders soft (not hiked), jaw unclenched.
Effect: Signals safety to nervous system, increases HRV, enables cognitive flexibility. Takes 30-60 seconds to shift autonomic state.
When to use: Doorway before entering room, after perturbation, anytime you notice physical bracing.
Noticing Patterns
Collapsed (forward head, rounded shoulders): Signals defeat, increases rumination. Notice this → gently lengthen spine.
Braced (jaw tight, shoulders hiked): Signals threat vigilance, narrows attention. Notice this → soften jaw, drop shoulders 1cm.

Each micro-adjustment is a mental "corrective step"—you are literally practicing physical equanimity.
Mini-practice
Choose one doorway today as your "posture gate." Every time you cross it, do a 10-second stance reset: feet, spine, chest, shoulders, jaw.

4-step recovery loop
  • 1. Detect (proprioception): Notice you've been knocked off-center ("I'm tipping into anger").
  • 2. Pause (compensatory step): Interrupt the momentum—breath, posture reset, neutral phrase.
  • 3. Control velocity: Prevent runaway thoughts/feelings—label story, let it pass without feeding it.
  • 4. Re-center: Return attention to breath/body, reconnect to values ("Why am I here? To reduce suffering."), resume action.

Track your recovery speed weekly—this is your primary progress metric.
